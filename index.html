About Barsana

About Barsana Barsana is a sacred town located in the Mathura district of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is revered as the divine birthplace of Shri Radha Rani, the eternal consort of Lord Shri Krishna. Nestled among the beautiful hills of Braj, Barsana is one of the holiest pilgrimage destinations for devotees from across India and around the world. The town is famous for the Shri Radha Rani Temple (Ladli Ji Temple), situated on the sacred Bhanugarh Hill. Every day, thousands of devotees visit the temple to seek the blessings of Shri Ji and experience the peaceful spiritual atmosphere. Barsana is also world-famous for its unique Lathmar Holi, celebrated with great enthusiasm every year. Other major festivals include Radhashtami, Jhulan Utsav, Janmashtami, and Kartik Mahotsav, which attract a large number of pilgrims. Apart from the temple, visitors can explore many sacred places such as Prem Sarovar, Kirti Mandir, Maan Mandir, Sankari Khor, Mor Kutir, and other important sites connected with the divine pastimes of Shri Radha and Shri Krishna. Surrounded by the natural beauty of Braj, Barsana offers a peaceful environment, devotional culture, and a unique spiritual experience. Every corner of the town reflects the love and devotion associated with Shri Radha Rani, making it one of the most cherished places for devotees. Shri Radha Rani Temple

The Shri Radha Rani Temple, also known as Ladli Ji Temple, is the most famous temple in Barsana and is situated on the sacred Bhanugarh Hill. It is dedicated to Shri Radha Rani, who is worshipped here as Ladli Ji. Devotees from across India and around the world visit this holy temple throughout the year to seek the blessings of Shri Radha Rani. The temple is especially crowded during Radhashtami, Lathmar Holi, Jhulan Utsav, Janmashtami, and other major festivals. The peaceful atmosphere, beautiful hilltop location, and devotional traditions make the temple one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Braj. Visitors are requested to maintain cleanliness, respect the temple rules, and help preserve the spiritual environment while visiting the temple.
